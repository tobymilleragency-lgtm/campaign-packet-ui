# Creative

## Non-negotiable rule
Real project photos can be enhanced and used as real work only when the original photo is real. AI images are for lifestyle, aspiration, and concept creative only. Never label an AI image as a completed job.

## Launch format
Start with 3 to 5 image ad variants. Do not launch with generated video.

## Image prompts
### Concept 1 — aspiration
A clean, well-maintained suburban home with a brand-new asphalt-shingle roof under a clear sky, warm natural light, professional real-estate photography style, inviting and trustworthy, no people.

### Concept 2 — storm worry
A modest American home after a storm, a few shingles visibly displaced, overcast sky clearing, realistic documentary photography style, conveying the question "is my roof okay?", not dramatic, not fake.

### Concept 3 — relief and trust
A relaxed homeowner standing in front of their house looking calm and reassured, soft natural light, authentic candid style, conveying peace of mind and good advice.

### Concept 4 — repair or replace decision
A roof close-up showing honest wear, suitable for a text overlay about repair or replacement, neutral and informative, realistic but not luxury-perfect.

## Design step overlays
Add the ad headline during the design step, not inside the generated image when possible. Keep overlay copy short and legible on mobile.

## Real photo enhancement workflow
If Toby provides real project photos, use image-to-image edits for crop, color, clarity, and ad-safe composition. Do not materially change the roof outcome. Do not imply Relax Remodel performed the construction.

## Variant plan
Generate 3 to 5 variants per concept, then pick the strongest 3 to 5 for launch testing.
