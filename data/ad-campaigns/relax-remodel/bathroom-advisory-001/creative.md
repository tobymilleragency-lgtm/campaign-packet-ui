# Creative

## Creative rule
Use AI images for lifestyle/concept visuals only. Do not imply an AI-generated bathroom is a real completed project.

## Best first image directions
1. Calm homeowner reviewing bathroom plans at a kitchen table, warm natural light, notebook and tile samples, trustworthy advisory mood.
2. Close-up of a remodel checklist beside simple bathroom material samples, practical and grounded, no contractor tools dominating the image.
3. Split concept image: confusing bid paperwork on one side, clear homeowner checklist on the other. Graphic/concept style, not fake project proof.

## Prompt — lifestyle concept
A realistic editorial photo of a homeowner at a kitchen table reviewing bathroom remodel notes, tile samples, and a simple checklist, warm natural daylight, trustworthy advisory mood, Midwest home, natural colors, no logos, no contractor branding, not a finished project claim, high quality but believable.

## Prompt — project review concept
An overhead photo of bathroom remodel planning materials: simple floor sketch, tile sample, faucet finish sample, handwritten questions for contractor, coffee cup, clean table, honest advice before hiring, warm neutral tones, realistic, not luxury-perfect.

## Real photo enhancement instructions
If Toby provides real homeowner/project photos, use image-to-image edits for crop, lighting, clarity, mild declutter, and ad-safe composition. Do not materially alter the project outcome or make it look like Relax Remodel performed the work.

## Compliance / trust notes
- Never say “our remodel” unless it is a real Relax Remodel case study and the advisory role is clear.
- Never present AI images as completed client work.
- Prefer advisory captions over transformation claims.
